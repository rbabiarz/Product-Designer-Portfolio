01 · Overview

INTELLIGENCE ISN'T SCARCE. Attention Is.

A watch-floor analyst doesn't lack data, they're drowning in it. Signals arrive faster than any team can read, each with its own caveats, contradictions and clocks. The hard part was never collecting the signal. It was deciding what matters, defending why, and being able to show your work, under time pressure, often on a degraded link.

My mandate was to design how decision intelligence should live on the floor, not a smarter black box, but a surface where AI compresses the reading, never the decision. Three principles held the system together: a fused picture, auditable reasoning, and work that stays resilient and releasable.

03 · Fusion & Reasoning

THE AI BRINGS RECEIPTS, Not Verdicts

The analyst never has to trust a number; they read the trace, follow any claim to its raw artefact, and decide.

04 · The Assessment

CONFIDENCE AND DISSENT, Made First-Class

An assessment leads with the bottom line, then carries every key judgement with its confidence level, its citations, and, crucially, any recorded dissent. Tradecraft standards (ICD 203) aren't decoration here: a peer reviewer's disagreement travels with the judgement it challenges, and the editor surfaces AI assists that strengthen or weaken each call.

05 · The Disconnected Edge

THE LINK WILL DROP. The Work Can't.

Forward teams operate DDIL, disconnected, degraded, intermittent, limited. AEGIS treats a 256 kbps satellite link, or no link at all, as a normal state. Entity resolution and drafting run on-device; everything queues and reconciles when the link returns. The hardest moment, a merge conflict where two people edited the same field while apart, is staged as an explicit human decision, side by side, never silently overwritten.

06 · Releasability

SHARING WITH ALLIES IS A WORKFLOW, Not a Copy-Paste

Sending an assessment to Five Eyes partners used to mean a nervous manual scrub. AEGIS proposes redactions inline on the document, substitute a collection method, redact an analyst's name, keep a disclosure-rounded figure, each with a reason and a one-tap accept or override, co-signed before release. And it protects the irreducible core: a recorded dissent can't be quietly dropped, because removing it would invalidate the assessment.

07 · Geospatial Intelligence, GIMS

THE MAP STOPS BEING A LAYER, It Becomes the Floor

The Geospatial Intelligence Map System turns the fused picture spatial. Every indicator, asset and sensor lives on one Common Operating Picture; operators draw geofences that watch for movement, replay the last 72 hours on a scrubbable timeline, and read an AI threat score on every track. The hard parts of a defence map, zones that trigger alerts, a temporal engine you can rewind, and a surface that keeps working air-gapped are the design, not the decoration.

6 zone types
Alert, exclusion, threat, surveillance, mission and engagement, drawn as a polygon, circle or rectangle straight onto the map.
8 trigger rules
Enter, exit, dwell time, speed, loitering, route deviation, each fires a classified, prioritised alert to the watch floor.
8 fused layers
Satellite, terrain, maritime AIS, aviation ADS-B, military tracks, intel overlays, sensor feeds and weather, toggled per role.

WHAT WORKED

Anchoring AI to the analyst's real burden, the firehose, gave it an obvious job. Framing every model output as a claim with receipts rather than an answer kept trust high: confidence is a starting point for judgement, and the evidence against a call is always in view.

WHAT I'D PUSH FURTHER

Provenance deserves to go deeper, a visible chain from raw collection to judgement to released product. And the reconcile model from the edge could become the spine of the whole system, so every workspace behaves correctly whether the floor is connected or dark.

"The win was never a smarter model. It was a surface where a person can move fast and still defend every call they made."
