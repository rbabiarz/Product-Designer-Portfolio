// Photometric map
Light the site, keep it even.

Every cell shows calculated illuminance in foot-candles (fc) — the light your GALN Galleon II area luminaires land on Stat Area 1, after a 0.912 light-loss factor. Drag a luminaire, click empty ground to add one, double-click to remove it; the field recomputes live.

Read the numbers
AVGMean illuminance across the stat area. RP-8-22 is driven by the minimum, not the average — a compliant lot typically averages 1–3 fc.
MAXThe brightest cell, directly under a pole. Peaks several times the average are normal in a parking lot — uniformity is judged by MAX:MIN, not by MAX alone.
MINThe darkest cell — the design's weak point. IES RP-8-22 requires ≥ 0.2 fc maintained at grade for parking lots and drive aisles.
MAX:MINUniformity: maximum ÷ minimum. IES RP-8-22 allows up to 20:1 for parking lots — lower is more uniform.
AVG:MINAverage ÷ minimum — the uniformity commercial design guides judge; the shipped Light Architect reports both ratios.
RP-8-22Basic requirements, parking lots at grade (not security lighting): all lots & drive aisles 0.2 fc min at 20:1; transaction areas 0.9 fc pre-curfew / 0.2 post at 15:1. Chosen over IES RP-6-20 (sports fields) and the Lighting Handbook dealer-lot tables because this site is an open customer parking lot at grade.
Why it matters

Targets follow IES RP-8-22, basic requirements for parking lots at grade (not security lighting): ≥ 0.2 fc maintained minimum and ≤ 20:1 max:min uniformity. Transaction areas call for 0.9 fc pre-curfew / 0.2 post-curfew at 15:1, and project specifics may necessitate different criteria. Values compute live — move a fixture and watch MAX:MIN respond.
