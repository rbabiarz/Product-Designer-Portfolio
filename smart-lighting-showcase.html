The Brief

Enterprise depth, consumer ease

The strategic opportunity: take enterprise-grade lighting technology, mesh networks, circadian tuning, fleet management, and make it feel as simple as a single switch, to compete directly in the consumer smart-home market.

Enterprise users tolerate friction; consumers abandon it. Every flow had to hide the complexity without removing the power.

Challenge

Power without complexity

Three tensions shaped every screen, each a constraint the design had to resolve, not a trade-off to accept.

01
Consumers abandon friction
The daily essentials must be reachable in one tap; everything configuration-related stays hidden until asked for.
02
Topology is intimidating
Users don't need to understand a mesh network, they need to trust that it just works, at any size.
03
Install is a chore
Commissioning had to happen before anything is mounted, scan a device in its box, assign it, done.

Approach

Making Network Topology Human

How do you hand BLE mesh configuration to someone who's never heard of BLE?

Users don't need to understand the technology if they can read the map. Black = active repeater, white = stable relay, red = reliability problem. We proposed four models, click through them — P1 is rebuilt live in the frame.
P1 · Node Map — rebuilt live, drag a bulb onto the gateway
P1 · NODE MAP DROPPED
Live mesh, drag-to-repeater

A literal node graph, drag any device onto the Gateway to promote it to a repeater, with red nodes flagging low reliability. Powerful, but it asked homeowners to think like network engineers. The frame here is a working rebuild. Drag a flagged bulb onto the gateway, then press TEST.

Device Distribution at Scale

The same logical map scales from a 32-device condo to a 247-device estate, groups, subnets, and repeaters assigned automatically. Example: 247 devices, 66 repeaters, 22 subnets created.

Auto-grouping across real floor plans
